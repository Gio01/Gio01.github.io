About Me

I am CyberSecurity Engineer working at a financial company with a focus on Identity Management. However my experience in the professional security space includes scripting, writing security tools/software, and analyzing priviledge access of users within environments.

I am also a recent InfoSec Graduate from Purdue University. During my last year at Purdue, I published my disertation "Comparing Social Engineering Training in the Context of Healthcare" where I conducted a study on the effectivenes of three different types of teaching methods which could be used to teach about Social Engineering concepts. The purpose of doing this was to find a single teaching method that would be more effective so that it could be used to teach not only people in the medical field but really anyone to not be as supcetible to Social Engineering attacks. Social Engineering attacks are the most successful cyber attacks and hence in doing this study we could aid people in various Industries to not fall as victim to such threats.

During my last year at DePauw, I published my thesis "Cyber Security in the Healthcare Industry" where I researched the many vectors of attack that exist within hospital settings. This included topics such as Medical Devices, Social Engineering, and Networking. From the research I had done on this thesis I decided that I wanted to learn more about security and hence I decided to pursue my current Master's in Information Security to learn more about security. From my experience, there is a gap between the development of software for functionality and the development of software with security in mind. I want to bridge this gap to help make software both secure and functional for users to use safely.
